# Business Finance Planning

A business financial planning system built with Google Spreadsheets to manage annual income projections, monthly cash flow, expenses, and financial forecasting.

## Overview

This system is designed for businesses with:
- Known or projected annual income
- Variable monthly revenue patterns
- Need for cash flow management
- Quarterly and annual financial planning

## Components

### Annual Revenue Projection
- Total annual income target
- Revenue breakdown by quarter
- Monthly distribution estimates
- Actual vs. projected tracking

### Monthly Cash Flow
- Expected monthly income (estimated from annual)
- Fixed and variable expenses
- Cash flow forecast
- Liquidity management

### Expense Management
- Operating expenses (payroll, rent, utilities)
- Variable costs (materials, services)
- Capital expenditures
- Tax provisions

### Profit & Loss
- Revenue recognition
- Cost of goods sold (COGS)
- Operating expenses
- Net profit margins

### Financial Metrics
- Burn rate and runway
- Break-even analysis
- Working capital
- Key performance indicators (KPIs)

## Getting Started

1. Create a Google Spreadsheet with separate sheets for each component
2. Define annual revenue targets and assumptions
3. Break down annual income into quarterly/monthly estimates
4. List all fixed and variable expenses
5. Set up cash flow projections
6. Monitor actuals vs. projections monthly
7. Adjust forecasts based on actual performance

## Spreadsheet Structure

- **Dashboard** - Key financial metrics and charts
- **Annual Plan** - Yearly revenue targets and breakdown
- **Monthly Forecast** - Estimated monthly income distribution
- **Cash Flow** - Monthly cash inflows and outflows
- **Expenses** - Detailed expense tracking by category
- **P&L Statement** - Profit and loss by period
- **Actuals vs. Plan** - Variance analysis
